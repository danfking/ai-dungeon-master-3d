## Part 1: The Big Picture (Author's World Bible)

### The Core Premise

You were an artist. A painter who found solace in watercolors—the gentle bleed of pigments, the way colors merged at their edges like old friends embracing. Then one night, while finishing a painting of a place that existed only in your dreams, you felt the brush *pull*. The colors swirled. The paper became a doorway.

You woke in the **Painted Realm**—a world that exists because artists dream it into being. Every stroke of every brush across every canvas in the mortal world adds to this place. It is beautiful beyond words.

But something is wrong.

A **Bleaching** spreads across the realm. Where vibrant paintings once stood, only gray ash remains. Creatures of void—beings that exist in the spaces *between* colors—have begun consuming the world itself.

And somehow, impossibly, you can fight back. You can *extract* the essence of color and wield it as magic.

---

### The Painted Realm - World Structure

The Painted Realm is organized into **Galleries**—vast regions shaped by different artistic traditions, emotions, and color palettes. Each Gallery was dreamed into existence by generations of artists, and each has its own character.

```
THE PAINTED REALM
├── The Sunlit Gallery (Warm colors, hope, beginnings)
│   └── Level 1-10 content - FIRST ARC
│
├── The Twilight Promenade (Cool colors, melancholy, transition)
│   └── Level 11-20 content
│
├── The Crimson Wing (Reds, passion, conflict, war)
│   └── Level 21-30 content
│
├── The Abyssal Collection (Dark colors, fear, depth)
│   └── Level 31-40 content
│
├── The Prismatic Archive (All colors, truth, revelation)
│   └── Level 41-50 content
│
└── The Bleached Void (No color, the source of corruption)
    └── Endgame content
```

### The Source of The Bleaching

**The Forgotten Artist**

Long ago, an artist of immense power attempted to paint "pure white"—a color that contained all light, all possibility. But to create absolute white, he had to *unmake* all other colors. He became the first Bleacher, and his obsession tore a hole between the Painted Realm and the Void Between Colors.

Through that tear, the Void leaks in. It is not malevolent—it is simply *absence*. It is what exists where no color has ever been imagined. And it is hungry for existence.

The Bleaching creatures are not evil. They are emptiness given form, desperately trying to *be* by consuming what already is.

**The Player's Unique Nature**

Other artists have been pulled into the Painted Realm before. Most become part of it—brushstrokes in someone else's painting. But you are different. You arrived at the exact moment when the Bleaching reached a critical mass, and something in the realm's desperation *awakened* you.

You are a **Chromancer**—one who can extract color without being absorbed by it, who can wield the essence of art as a weapon. You may be the realm's last hope.

### The Five Books (Full Series Arc)

**Book 1: "The Fading Light" (Levels 1-10)**
*The Sunlit Gallery*
- Player awakens in the warmest, most hopeful part of the realm
- Learns that the Bleaching is accelerating
- Discovers they're a Chromancer
- Confronts the first Major Void Entity: **The Pallid One**
- Revelation: The Bleaching has a source—and it's trying to reach the mortal world

**Book 2: "The Dying Dusk" (Levels 11-20)**
*The Twilight Promenade*
- Player ventures into regions of sadness and memory
- Meets other survivors—artists who've become living paintings
- Learns the history of the Forgotten Artist
- Confronts: **The Shade of Sorrow**
- Revelation: The Void contains trapped artists who didn't survive the transition

**Book 3: "The Burning Canvas" (Levels 21-30)**
*The Crimson Wing*
- War between remaining Gallery factions
- Some want to fight the Bleaching; others want to surrender to it
- Player must unite the factions
- Confronts: **The Ember Lord** (a Chromancer who fell to rage)
- Revelation: Chromancers can *become* Bleaching creatures if corrupted

**Book 4: "The Deepening Gray" (Levels 31-40)**
*The Abyssal Collection*
- Player descends into the oldest, darkest parts of the realm
- Discovers the first paintings ever dreamed—cave art, ancient symbols
- Meets the original Guardians of the Realm
- Confronts: **The Ancient Absence**
- Revelation: The Void has a mind. It is the Forgotten Artist, transformed.

**Book 5: "The Final Stroke" (Levels 41-50)**
*The Prismatic Archive & The Bleached Void*
- All colors unite for final stand
- Player must reach the Forgotten Artist's original canvas
- Either destroy him (and the Void) or redeem him
- Multiple endings based on how much color was preserved vs. consumed
- Final Confrontation: **The Forgotten Artist / The White Absolute**

## Part 5: Art Direction

### Core Visual Style: Ink & Watercolor

**The Signature Look**: All art in Chromatic Resonance uses an **ink outline with watercolor fill** style—similar to traditional illustration or concept art. This creates:
- Clear, readable silhouettes from the ink linework
- Rich, atmospheric color from watercolor washes
- A hand-crafted, painterly quality that reinforces the "Painted Realm" setting

**Style Keywords for All Prompts**:
```
"ink and watercolor illustration, black ink outlines, watercolor washes,
visible brushstrokes, paper texture, traditional media look"
```

### Enemy Sprite Rendering - The Bleeding Edge Technique

**Problem**: Placing humanoid enemy sprites over room backgrounds creates visual issues:
- Sprites appear to "float" if not positioned at exact floor level
- Different rooms have different perspective/floor positions
- Multiple enemies in one room creates layering complexity

**Solution**: All enemy sprites use a "bleeding watercolor fade" at the bottom edge.

**Visual Effect**:
- Bottom 20-30% of each sprite fades to transparency via watercolor bleeding
- Ink outlines dissolve into dripping ink lines
- Colors appear to drip, dissolve, or bleed downward like wet paint on paper
- No hard bottom edge - sprites naturally blend into any surface
- Creates an ethereal "manifesting from the canvas" or "hovering apparition" feel

**Lore Justification**:
These are painted creatures - dreams given form by artists' brushstrokes. The Bleaching is causing them to lose cohesion, their colors literally draining away. The bleeding effect represents their unstable existence, caught between being and unbeing.

**Technical Benefits**:
1. **Universal placement** - Same sprite works in any room regardless of floor position
2. **Multi-enemy support** - Overlapping sprites with soft edges blend naturally
3. **No positioning logic** - Simplifies rendering code
4. **Aesthetic consistency** - Reinforces the ink & watercolor medium throughout

**Prompt Template for Enemies**:
```
"ink and watercolor illustration, black ink outlines with watercolor washes,
[subject description], bottom of figure fading into watercolor bleeding effect,
ink lines dissolving into drips, colors dissolving downward into transparency,
masterpiece quality, transparent background"
```

**Prompt Template for Rooms**:
```
"ink and watercolor illustration, black ink linework with watercolor washes,
first person perspective, [room description], visible brushstrokes,
paper texture, traditional media, masterpiece quality"
```

**Negative Prompt Addition**:
```
"hard edges at bottom, solid feet on ground, sharp cutoff, clean bottom edge,
photorealistic, 3d render, digital art, anime, no outlines"
```

## Summary

This plan approaches Chromatic Resonance as **Book 1 of a 5-book series**, establishing:

1. **The World**: The Painted Realm, organized into Galleries dreamed by artists
2. **The Threat**: The Bleaching, caused by the Forgotten Artist seeking pure white
3. **The Arc**: From humble beginnings in the Sunlit Gallery to confronting the source
4. **The Starter Zone**: Warmwood Meadow → Faded Orchard (Levels 1-5)
5. **Themed Enemies**: Corrupted children's drawings and orchard creatures, not generic undead

The enemies reflect the zone's nature—innocent art corrupted by absence—rather than traditional fantasy monsters. This creates emotional resonance with the color-extraction theme.
